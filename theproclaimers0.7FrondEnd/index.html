The Proclaimer whisky

The Proclaimer is a Whisky fabricated at the highlands in Scotland since 1983, discovered by the hands of 5 adventurers from Portugal that were pioneers at the craft of whisky making and programming.

After 500 miles and a 1000 more traveling to find a wonderful beverage made from the best grains and the purest water Scotland has to offer, the courageous adventurers ended up making this marvelous and harmonious whisky combination.

Later in history these brave men founded a company in order to proclaim the good name of such quality beverages, with a transport chain and a website development company (“Team Burners Lee”) where they offer these services together and sometimes even sing along with “The proclaimers” a well known band from Scotland. Malting : The Starting Point of Malt Whisky Production

Barley is one of the three vital ingredients required to create whisky. The malting process, involves the barley being steeped in water and spread across a malting floor to be allowed to germinate – this then provides the starch which turns into a soluble sugar that can be used to create alcohol. The process, which takes around a week, ends with the drying of the barley in a kiln, a large oven. Here is where smoky whiskies get their smokiness as when peat is used to burn it provides that unique smoky flavour.

Mashing: The Second Stage of Whisky Production

After the malting process and when the barley has been dried it is ground up in a mill. The crushed grain now identified as grist is mixed with hot water in a mash tun.The mixing allows the sugars to dissolve and we are left with a sweet liquid, identified as wort, which can be turned into alcohol.

Fermentation : The Third Stage of Whisky Production

The mash is added to a fermentation tank, along with yeast. The yeast converts the sugar to alcohol. After three or four days, the resulting liquid is about 10% alcoholic and is known as distiller's beer, or wash.

Distillation: The Fourth Stage of Whisky Production

The wash is heated to the point where the alcohol turns to vapor, but the water remains liquid. The alcohol is then collected in a second container. This process is repeated to produce “high wine” or “new whisky”.

Maturation: The final Stage of Whisky Production

Another key element of the process, where the new make spirit is placed in the cask to be aged. To be called Scotch Whisky it will need be aged in an oak cask for a minimum of three years. The cask will give the Whisky its colour among other distinctive characteristics.
